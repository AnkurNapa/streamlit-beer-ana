# Master Brewer - Complete Feature Guide

Inspired by beer-analytics.com, this comprehensive brewing assistant includes 9 interactive pages with advanced analysis tools.

## 📱 Pages Overview

### 🎯 1. Quick Hop Finder
**Fast hop recommendations based on beer characteristics**
- Input target IBU (bitterness), ABV (alcohol %), and beer style
- Get top 5 compatible hops instantly
- View flavor profiles and aroma characteristics
- See alpha acid percentages for each recommendation
- Perfect for quick decisions when brewing

**Use Cases:**
- "I want a 60 IBU IPA at 6.5% ABV"
- Quick style-based recommendations
- One-click hop suggestions

---

### 📊 2. Beer Style Explorer
**Browse all BJCP beer styles with auto-tuned hop recommendations**
- Select beer category (Lager, Ale, IPA, Stout, etc.)
- Choose specific sub-category (American IPA, Imperial Stout, etc.)
- See official style ranges:
  - IBU (bitterness) ranges
  - ABV (alcohol) ranges
  - SRM (color) ranges
  - OG (original gravity) ranges
- Get hop recommendations tailored to that exact style
- View alpha acid distribution
- Compare similar styles

**Use Cases:**
- Learning what defines a beer style
- Finding hops appropriate for specific styles
- Understanding style guidelines
- Comparing IPAs vs Pale Ales

**Precise hop amounts for different brewing stages**
- Select a hop and batch size
- Get recommended amounts for:
  - **Bittering (60 min):** Heavy alpha acid utilization
  - **Aroma (5-15 min):** Balanced flavor/aroma
  - **Dry Hop:** Post-fermentation flavor bomb
- **IBU Calculator:**
  - Enter bittering amount in ounces
  - Select boil time (10-90 minutes)
  - See estimated IBU contribution
  - Understand utilization rates

**Dosage Examples (per 5 gal batch):**
- Citra bittering: 1.0-1.5 oz
- Cascade aroma: 0.75-1.5 oz
- Nelson Sauvin dry hop: 1.0-2.0 oz

**Use Cases:**
- Calculating exact hop amounts
- Understanding IBU contributions
- Planning hop additions
- Scaling recipes up/down

## 🧠 Key Concepts

### Alpha Acids (AA)
- Measures hop bitterness potential
- Low: 3-5% (aroma hops)
- Medium: 5-10% (dual-purpose)
- High: 10%+ (bittering hops)

### IBU (International Bitterness Units)
- Measures bitterness from hops
- Mild: 0-30 IBU
- Medium: 30-60 IBU
- Bold: 60+ IBU

### Hop Type
- **Bittering:** High AA, used early (60 min boil)
- **Aroma:** Low AA, used late (5-15 min boil)
- **Dual-Purpose:** Medium AA, versatile use

### Dosage
- **Bittering:** 1.0-1.5 oz per 5 gal batch
- **Aroma:** 0.5-1.5 oz per 5 gal batch
- **Dry Hop:** 0.5-2.0 oz per 5 gal batch

## 💡 Pro Tips

### For IPAs
- Use high AA American hops (Citra, Simcoe, Mosaic)
- Bittering: 1.0-1.5 oz
- Dry hop: 1.5-2.0 oz
- Target 60-70 IBU

### For Lagers
- Use noble hops (Saaz, Hallertau)
- Lower IBU (20-40)
- Emphasis on balance

### For Stouts
- Medium AA hops (Fuggle, Goldings)
- Low IBU (30-40)
- Herbal/earthy notes complement roast

### For Pale Ales
- Cascade, Centennial, Amarillo
- Medium IBU (30-50)
- Floral + citrus balance
